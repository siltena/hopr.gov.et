A Brief History of Ethiopian Parliament
Parliamentary system had been effected in different regimes of Ethiopia. This text tries to describe features of the parliamentary systems introduced by the regimes ruled the country.
Parliament during the reign of Emperor Haile Sillasie ́ (Autocracy rule)-1930-74)
The first written constitution was introduced during the reign of Empror Haile Sillasie. The people did not participate in the draft as well as ratification of the constitution as Emperor conferred it to his people as a gift at his coronation, in 1931. The 1931 constitution established the first parliament in the history of the country. The parliament was bicameral legislature: Yehig Mewossagna Mikir Bet (the Senate) (Upper House) and Yehig Memria Mikir Bet (Chamber of Deputies) (Lower House).
The Emperor elected advisors of Yehig Mewossagna Mikir Bet who had long served the government as aristocracy, magistrate, minister and military leader. As regards of Yehig Memria Mikir Bet Advisors, they were supposed to have estate as a warranty. However, it is not permissible to the wealthy populace.
Each House had 56 members. Every bill would not be enacted as a law unless,the two Houses deliberated upon and the will of emperor heard. In Nov. 3, 1931, the Houses commenced their works in the current palace; two years later, shifted to the present building of the parliament.
After the end of Italian occupation, since 1943, 5 members from each provinces had been selected forYehig Mewossagna Mikir Bet ; in sum they were 60.By the decision of the Emperor, 30 members elected for Yehig Memria Mikir Bet. The number of MPs for the Houses was 90 ( 60 Yehig Mewossagna and 30 Yehig Memria) .The first constitution was in service for 24 years until amendment to it had been made in 1955.As a result of which, it was decided that the public to elect members of Yehig Memria Mikir Bet. Accordingly, 125 and 250 members had been elected for Yehig Memria and for Yehig Mewossagna respectively. The parliament transformed from advisory status to legislative.
It was after the revised constitution that standing committees were set up to scrutinize and lodge issues to the parliament for resolutions. The revised constitution shows that
2
there existed constitutional bodies: Council of the Crown, Council of Ministers, the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. The constitution revised again before the emperor removed from power on September 1, 1974; however, the emperor ousted from by military junta before the constitution came in to effect. When the military dictatorial Dergue assumed state power through coup d’état, dissolved the parliament.
The National Shengo( the Assembly) of the Dergue (military Junta)- 1974-91)
The Dergue, under the Provisional Military Government ruled the country for 13 years without constitution. The military junta transformed to a party system as it did set up workers party of Ethiopia (WPE) and purported to have been a civil government. The Dergue adopted a constitution in line with Marxist-Leninist ideology and proclaimed Ethiopian People’s Democratic Republic (EPDR) constitution on Sep. 12, 1987. The WPE ruled the country solely occupying the National Assembly seats. The National Assembly had 5 years tenure; it was established by members elected from 835 constituencies. According to the provision of the PDRE constitution, the Shengo conducted its sitting once a year; unto year, council of government sorted out issues to have been decided by the National Shengo. The Shengo was unicameral legislature oriented to socialist principle and based on one party rule; it did outlaw a multiparty system.
Ethiopian Peoples’ Revolutionary Democratic Front (Democratic Government)-1991---)
Council of the Transitional Government of Ethiopia( June 1991-July1995)
Following the down fall of the Dergue regime by the EPRDF in armed struggle, on May 1991, EPRDF called upon political forces that fought to topple down the Dergue.The Council of the Transitional Government of Ethiopia was formed as an outcome of Peace and Democratic Transition Conference of Ethiopia held in Addis Ababa from July 1-5, 19 91. The conference approved a Charter by which the Council directed. The charter set up House of Representatives and Council of Ministers. The House of Representatives was legislative; its members were from various political forces: political parties, movements and armed forces.
3
The House had 86 members, of which 3 were women. The content and mission of charter had geared to dissolute oppressive institutions of the former government, ensure the right and benefit of the disadvantaged group, redress as well as renounce the past predicament, and also succeed the transition programme. The House was in office for four years term; its main mission had been to arrange for the establishment of permanent democratic government. Accordingly, it did set up constitutional commission that prepared a draft constitution. The draft constitution augmented with public opinion and eventually on Dec. 8, 1994, the FDRE constitution has been adopted by the members of constitutional council elected by the people for this purpose. In conformity with the constitution, in May 1995, His Excellency Mr. Meles Zenawi, who had been president of Transitional Government, was elected, the FDRE prime minister. Peaceful and democratic transition of political power to the government elected by the people succeeded, and the transitional period came to an end. Parliament of the FDRE( Since1995-) The Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia (FDRE) has two Federal Houses in consistence with article 53 of the constitution: House of Peoples’ Representatives and House of the Federation. According to the provision of the FDRE constitution article 54, members of the House of peoples’ Representatives shall be elected by the people for a term of five years on the basis of universal suffrage and by direct, free and fair election held by secret ballot. Members of the House, on the basis of population and special representation of minority Nationalities and Peoples, shall not exceed 550; of these, minority Nationalities and Peoples shall have at least 20 seats. Particulars shall be determined by law. According to the FDRE constitution article 50(3), the House of Peoples’ Representatives is the highest authority of the Federal Government. The House is responsible to the people. The State Council is the highest organ of State authority. It is responsible to the people of the State.
According to the FDRE constitution article 55, the Powers and Functions of the House within jurisdiction of federal government described as follow:

1. The House of Peoples' Representatives shall have the Power of legislation in all matters assigned by the Constitution to Federal jurisdiction.
2. Consistent with the provision of sub-Article 1 of this Article, the House of Peoples' Representatives shall enact Specific laws on the following matters:
(a) Utilization of land and other natural resources, of rivers and lakes crossing the boundaries of the national territorial jurisdiction or linking two or more States;
(b) Inter-State commerce and foreign trade;
(c) Air, rail, water and sea transport, major roads linking two or more States, postal and telecommunication services;
(d) Enforcement of the political rights established by the Constitution and electoral laws and procedures;
(e) Nationality, immigration, passport, exit from and entry into the country, the rights of refugees and of asylum;
(f) Uniform standards of measurement and calendar;
(g) Patents and copyrights;
(h) The possession and bearing of arms.
3. It shall enact a labour code.
4. It shall enact a commercial code.
5. It shall enact a penal code. The States may, however, enact penal laws on matters that are not specifically covered by Federal penal legislation.
6. It shall enact civil laws which the House of the Federation deems necessary to establish and sustain one economic community.
7. It shall determine the organization of national defence, public security, and a national police force. If the conduct of these forces infringes upon human rights and the nation's security, it shall carry out investigations and take necessary measures.
8. In conformity with Article 93 of the Constitution it shall declare a state of emergency; it shall consider and resolve on a decree of a state of emergency declared by the executive.
5
9. On the basis of a draft law submitted to it by the Council of Ministers it shall proclaim a state of war.
10. It shall approve general policies and strategies of economic, social and development, and fiscal and monetary policy of the country. It shall. enact laws on matters relating to the local currency, the administration of the National Bank, and foreign exchange.
11. It shall levy taxes and duties on revenue sources reserved to the Federal Government, it shall ratify the Federal budget.
12. It shall ratify international agreements concluded by the Executive.
13. It shall approve the appointment of Federal judges, members of the Council of Ministers, Commissioners, the Auditor General, and of other officials whose appointment is required by law to be approved by it.
14. It shall establish a Human Rights Commission and determine by law its powers and functions.
15. It shall establish the institution of the Ombudsman, and select and appoint its members. It shall determine by law the powers and functions of the institution.
16. It shall, on its own initiative, request a joint session of the House of the Federation and of the House of Peoples' Representatives to take appropriate measures when State authorities are unable to arrest violations of human rights within their jurisdiction. It shall, on the basis of the joint decision of the House, give directives to the concerned State authorities.
17. It has the power to call and to question the Prime Minister and other Federal officials and to investigate the Executive's conduct and discharge of its responsibilities.
18. It shall, at the request of one-third of its members, discuss any matter pertaining to the powers of the executive. It has, in such cases, the power to take decisions or measures it deems necessary.
19.It shall elect the Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the House. It

shall establish standing and ad hoc committees as it deems necessary to accomplish its work.

Vision

House envisions being an institution of exemplary and developed multiparty parliamentary democracy in Africa by 2023

Mission

  • Legislating laws that contribute to the democratic system building, ensuring of good governance and peace, and the socio-economic development;

  • Following up and supervising the proper implementation of the laws, government polices and plans, and effective utilization of the resources allocated for executing the plans and policies thereby ensure the public is beneficial.

  • Organizing a procedure and a system that helps to consolidate the multiparty parliamentary democracy and ensure Just full and fair participation in the activities of the House.
